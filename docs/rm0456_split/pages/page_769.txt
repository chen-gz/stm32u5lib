LPDMA channel state and direct programming without
any linked-list
After a LPDMA reset, a LPDMA channel x is in idle state. When the software writes 1 into
the in LPDMA_CxCR.EN enable control bit, the channel takes into account the value of the
different channel configuration registers (LPDMA_CxXXX), switches to the active/non-idle
state and starts to execute the corresponding requested data transfers.
After enabling/starting a LPDMA channel transfer by writing 1 into the LPDMA_CxCR.EN
bit, a LPDMA channel interrupt on a complete transfer notifies the software that the LPDMA
channel is back in idle state (EN is then de-asserted by hardware) and that the channel is
ready to be reconfigured then enabled again.
